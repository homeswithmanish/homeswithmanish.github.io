Features

A closing-file operating system, not a generic document tool with a real-estate skin.

AI document extraction

Upload a tax certificate, CPS, or lender instruction letter and Conveyo runs OCR and pulls structured fields — parties, amounts, dates, parcel identifiers — into the matter. The extraction step and the OCR step are separate, swappable connectors, so the underlying document-AI provider can change without touching how a lawyer uses the feature.

Jurisdiction-aware tax calculators

Land transfer tax (plus municipal surtax where it applies, like Toronto), first-time-buyer rebates, and the federal/provincial GST-HST new-housing rebate are computed from the real bracket and exemption rules for each province — not a flat percentage. Every calculation posts directly as a line item on the statement of adjustments.

Per-province checklists and templates

Opening a matter seeds a closing checklist that combines universal conveyancing steps with the ones specific to that province — Teraview registration and Ontario land transfer tax disclosure in Ontario, Form A/B and LTSA submission in BC. Document templates follow the same rule: a purchase file in BC surfaces BC's forms, not Ontario's.

Trust accounting & statement of adjustments

Trust ledger, reconciliation, and the statement of adjustments live in the same system as the matter itself. Post the balance due on closing straight into the trust account in one action, with a full audit trail behind every entry.

ID verification & address validation

Connector integrations handle identity/AML verification and address validation, each with a real named-provider integration and a local-simulation fallback so the workflow still runs end-to-end before credentials are configured.

Matter-scoped AI assistant

Ask questions grounded only in a specific matter’s own indexed documents, draft a requisition letter or reporting letter from the matter’s actual data, and let deterministic calculators — not the model — compute the numbers those drafts cite.
